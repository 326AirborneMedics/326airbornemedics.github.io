The 326th in World War II

Activated in 1942, the 326th Airborne Medical Company served as the medical company of the 101st Airborne Division during World War II. Its personnel supported airborne troops through Normandy, Holland, Bastogne, and the final campaigns in Europe.

Our unit portrays these medical soldiers through living history, public education, and historically inspired displays.

Who We Are

The 326th Airborne Medical Company (Reenacted) is a World War II living history and reenactment unit dedicated to portraying the medical personnel who served with America’s airborne forces in the European Theater.

Through living history displays, public events, educational programs, and historically inspired field impressions, we work to keep the story of the airborne medic alive for future generations.

Interested in Joining?

Whether you're an experienced reenactor or completely new to living history, we welcome individuals who share our commitment to preserving the history and legacy of the airborne medic.

Join us as we preserve the past, educate the public, and honor the service of those who carried aid and hope into battle.
